Fractions

Topic id gmat::quant::arithmetic::fractions · Domain Arithmetic · Prereqs number properties (factors, LCM), whole-number division · ~22 min

Learning objectives

  • Add and subtract fractions using a common denominator; simplify results.
  • Multiply fractions and divide by multiplying by the reciprocal.
  • Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions.
  • Find a fraction 'of' a quantity, including a fraction of what remains.
  • Compare fractions quickly using a common denominator or benchmarks.

Model: I do → we do → you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Factors and LCM (for common denominators) and division.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

From memory, no calculator. 4 minutes.

  1. Write 18/24 in lowest terms.3/4 (divide top and bottom by 6).
  2. Compute 3/8 + 1/8.4/8 = 1/2.
  3. What is the LCM of 4 and 6?12.
  4. Convert 2 1/3 to an improper fraction.7/3.
  5. Why can't 1/3 + 1/4 equal 2/7?The pieces are different sizes; you must use a common denominator first.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Retrieves simplifying, same-denominator addition, and LCM — the prerequisites for adding unlike fractions and for the 'fraction of a quantity' problems the GMAT loves.
If students struggle: If LCM is shaky, recap with multiples lists (4: 4,8,12 …; 6: 6,12) before today's unlike-denominator work; the whole topic rides on it.

Prior-knowledge bridge

You added 3/8 + 1/8 easily because the pieces were the same size. The Do-Now's last question is the whole point of today: when denominators differ, you can't just add tops. You'll use the LCM skill you just retrieved to make the pieces the same size first — then multiply, divide, and take fractions of quantities the way GMAT word problems demand.

Learning intention

By the end you can add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions (including mixed numbers) and compute a fraction of a quantity — simplifying as you go.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can combine two unlike fractions by rewriting them over a common denominator.
  • I can divide fractions by multiplying by the reciprocal.
  • I can answer a 'fraction of a quantity' word problem in one clean setup.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
A jar is 3/4 full of water. If 1/3 of the water is poured out, what fraction of the jar is now filled?
  • A1/2
  • B5/12
  • C1/4
  • D7/12
  • E2/3

Think-aloud

  1. The jar starts at 3/4 full.
  2. Removing 1/3 of the water leaves 2/3 of the water.
  3. 'Of' means multiply: 2/3 × 3/4 = (2×3)/(3×4) = 6/12.
  4. Simplify 6/12 = 1/2. The jar is half full.

Correct: A. Pouring out 1/3 of the water leaves 2/3 of it. Remaining = 2/3 × 3/4 = 6/12 = 1/2 of the jar.

Takeaway. 'Of' means multiply; 'a fraction of what's left' multiplies the leftover fraction, not the original amount.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)easy
Compute 5/6 − 3/8.
  • A1/4
  • B11/24
  • C1/2
  • D13/24
  • E7/24
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Find the LCD of 6 and 8 — it's 24.
  2. Rewrite: 5/6 = 20/24, 3/8 = 9/24.
  3. Subtract the numerators over 24.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right — 20/24 − 9/24 = 11/24, already in lowest terms.
If incorrect: Don't subtract numerators and denominators separately. Convert both to /24 first.

Answer: B. LCD of 6 and 8 is 24: 5/6 = 20/24 and 3/8 = 9/24, so 20/24 − 9/24 = 11/24.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
What is 2/3 ÷ 4/9?
  • A8/27
  • B3/4
  • C3/2
  • D2/3
  • E6
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Keep–change–flip: dividing by 4/9 means multiplying by 9/4.
  2. 2/3 × 9/4. Cancel the 3 into the 9 first.
  3. = 2 × 3 / 4 = 6/4 = 3/2.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes — reciprocal then simplify gives 3/2.
If incorrect: 8/27 is the trap from multiplying instead of inverting. Flip the divisor: × 9/4.

Answer: C. Dividing by a fraction = multiplying by its reciprocal: 2/3 ÷ 4/9 = 2/3 × 9/4 = 18/12 = 3/2.

Application-first — attempt before revealing

On a test, 3/5 of the questions are quantitative and 1/4 of the remaining questions are geometry. What fraction of all the questions are geometry?
  • A1/10
  • B1/20
  • C3/20
  • D2/9
  • E1/5
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Remaining after quant = 1 − 3/5 = 2/5. Geometry = 1/4 × 2/5 = 2/20 = 1/10.
