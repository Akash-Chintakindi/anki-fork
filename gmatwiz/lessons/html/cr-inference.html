Critical Reasoning: Inference

Topic id gmat::verbal::cr::inference · Domain Critical Reasoning · Prereqs read a set of statements precisely, distinguish 'must be true' from 'could be true' · ~18 min

Learning objectives

  • Choose the statement that MUST be true given the premises - nothing more.
  • Combine conditional statements and use the contrapositive.
  • Reject choices that go even slightly beyond what the text guarantees.

Model: I do -> we do -> you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Reading conditional ('if-then') statements and their contrapositives.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

No notes. 4 minutes.

  1. Contrapositive of 'If it rains, the game is canceled.'If the game is not canceled, it did not rain.
  2. Does 'most A are B' let you conclude 'most B are A'?No.
  3. 'Must be true' vs 'could be true' - which does inference require?Must be true.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Recalls the contrapositive and the strict 'must be true' bar - the backbone of inference questions.
If students struggle: Have them cross out any choice that adds a new idea not in the text.

Prior-knowledge bridge

Unlike strengthen/weaken, inference gives you the premises as true and asks what they force. Your job isn't to judge an argument - it's to find the one choice the statements guarantee, using chaining and the contrapositive, and to reject anything that reaches beyond the text.

Learning intention

By the end you can select the only choice that must be true given the statements, combining conditionals and rejecting overreach.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can take the premises as given and not add outside assumptions.
  • I can chain conditionals and apply the contrapositive.
  • I can eliminate 'could be true but not guaranteed' choices.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Every product in the store's front display is on sale. Nothing that is on sale can be returned for a refund. If the statements above are true, which of the following must also be true?
  • ANo product in the front display can be returned for a refund.
  • BEvery product that cannot be returned is in the front display.
  • CProducts not on sale are all in the back of the store.
  • DSome products in the front display can be returned.
  • EThe store rarely gives refunds.

Think-aloud

  1. Premise 1: front display -> on sale. Premise 2: on sale -> no refund.
  2. Chain them: front display -> on sale -> no refund.
  3. So every front-display product cannot be refunded - that's A.
  4. B reverses the chain; C, D, E add facts the text never gives.

Correct: A. Front-display items are all on sale, and on-sale items cannot be refunded; chaining these, no front-display item can be refunded. A is forced; the others reverse the logic or add new claims.

Takeaway. Inference = what the premises force. Chain conditionals forward; never reverse them or add outside information.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)easy
All of the interns attended the safety briefing. Anyone who attended the safety briefing received a badge. If the statements above are true, which of the following must be true?
  • AEvery intern received a badge.
  • BOnly interns received badges.
  • CEveryone with a badge is an intern.
  • DSome interns did not attend the briefing.
  • EThe briefing was required by law.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Write the two conditionals and chain them.
  2. intern -> attended -> badge.
  3. Beware choices that flip the arrow ('everyone with a badge is an intern').
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right - chaining gives intern -> badge.
If incorrect: B and C reverse the logic; the text only guarantees intern -> badge.

Answer: A. Interns -> attended -> received a badge, so every intern got a badge. The reversed and added-fact choices aren't guaranteed.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
No book in the reference room may be borrowed. Every atlas in this library is kept in the reference room. If the statements above are true, which of the following must be true?
  • ANo atlas in this library may be borrowed.
  • BEvery book that may not be borrowed is an atlas.
  • CAll borrowable books are outside the reference room.
  • DSome atlases may be borrowed.
  • EThe reference room contains only atlases.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. atlas -> in reference room -> cannot be borrowed.
  2. Chain forward to the guaranteed result.
  3. Reject choices that reverse or add claims.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Exactly - the chain forces 'no atlas may be borrowed.'
If incorrect: C reverses the relationship; only atlas -> not borrowable is guaranteed.

Answer: A. Atlases are in the reference room, and reference-room books cannot be borrowed; so no atlas can be borrowed. The rest reverse the logic or overreach.

4You do — independent application

Application-first (SPOV2): commit to an answer, then reveal the explanation. Log every miss.

you_1 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

The theater sells out every show that opens on a Saturday. Last week's show did not sell out. If the statements above are true, which of the following must be true?
  • ALast week's show did not open on a Saturday.
  • BThe theater never opens shows on Saturdays.
  • CLast week's show was poorly reviewed.
  • DThe theater will sell out its next Saturday show.
  • EMost of the theater's shows open on Saturdays.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Opening on Saturday guarantees a sellout; since the show did not sell out, by the contrapositive it did not open on a Saturday.

  • DMaria will be invited later.

Application-first — attempt before revealing

All the vehicles in the company fleet run on electricity. No electric vehicle in the city is allowed in the old tunnel. If the statements above are true, which of the following must be true?
  • ANo vehicle in the company fleet is allowed in the old tunnel.
  • BEvery vehicle barred from the tunnel is in the company fleet.
  • CThe company fleet is the largest in the city.
  • DSome fleet vehicles are allowed in the tunnel.
  • EElectric vehicles are cheaper to operate.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Fleet vehicles are electric, and city electric vehicles are barred from the tunnel; so no fleet vehicle is allowed in the tunnel. The rest reverse or overreach.

5Mastery check

A topic is mastered at >=85% correct on new/held-out-style questions across >=2 spaced sessions with delay (PRD Section 9.2). Flashcard recall alone is not mastery; application under mixed, delayed conditions is the bar.

Threshold 0.85 · spaced sessions ≥ 2
