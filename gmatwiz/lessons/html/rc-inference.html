Reading Comprehension: Inference

Topic id gmat::verbal::rc::inference · Domain Reading Comprehension · Prereqs read a passage precisely, distinguish what is stated from what is implied · ~18 min

Learning objectives

  • Choose the option that must be true given the passage - no outside knowledge.
  • Prefer small, well-supported inferences over large leaps.
  • Reject options that go beyond, distort, or contradict the passage.

Model: I do -> we do -> you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Telling a stated fact from an implied one.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

No notes. 4 minutes.

  1. An RC inference must be what, relative to the passage?Strongly supported / must be true given the text.
  2. May you use outside knowledge on inference questions?No - only the passage.
  3. Are big, dramatic inferences usually right on the GMAT?No - the GMAT rewards small, safe inferences.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Recalls the strict 'supported by the passage only' bar for inference.
If students struggle: Have them point to the exact sentence that supports a candidate inference.

Prior-knowledge bridge

You can tell stated from implied. An inference question asks for the implied claim the passage makes almost inevitable - the smallest safe step beyond the words, supported by a specific sentence, never a leap that needs outside facts.

Learning intention

By the end you can select the choice the passage most supports and reject leaps beyond it.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can point to the sentence that supports my inference.
  • I can prefer a small, safe inference over a dramatic one.
  • I can reject options that add outside information.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Passage: The town's only bridge is closed for repairs until spring. During the closure, the ferry is the sole way to cross the river, and it does not run after dark. Question: It can be inferred from the passage that, during the closure,
  • Athere is no way to cross the river after dark
  • Bthe bridge will never reopen
  • Cthe ferry is faster than the bridge was
  • Dmost residents rarely need to cross the river
  • Ethe ferry runs more often than it did before

Think-aloud

  1. Facts: ferry is the only crossing; ferry stops after dark.
  2. Combine: after dark, no crossing at all.
  3. A is forced; B/C/D/E need outside information.

Correct: A. The ferry is the SOLE crossing and doesn't run after dark, so during the closure there is no after-dark crossing. A is a small, forced inference; the others add facts the passage never gives.

Takeaway. Combine the passage's own statements into the smallest claim they force - nothing extra.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)medium
Passage: Every painting in the exhibit was completed after 1950. The museum's oldest painting was completed in 1600. Question: The passage most strongly supports which of the following?
  • AThe museum's oldest painting is not in the exhibit.
  • BThe exhibit contains the museum's most valuable paintings.
  • CPaintings from 1600 are rare.
  • DThe exhibit has more paintings than the rest of the museum.
  • EThe museum was founded after 1950.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Compare the dates: exhibit is post-1950; the oldest is 1600.
  2. A pre-1950 painting cannot be in a post-1950 exhibit.
  3. Reject options needing value, rarity, or founding date.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right - the date rule excludes the 1600 painting.
If incorrect: Value and rarity aren't in the passage; only dates are.

Answer: A. Exhibit paintings are all post-1950; the 1600 painting is pre-1950, so it can't be in the exhibit. A is forced; the rest add unsupported claims.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
Passage: The clinic accepts walk-in patients only on weekday mornings. On weekday afternoons and all weekend, patients must have an appointment. Question: It can be inferred that a patient without an appointment
  • Acannot be seen on a Saturday
  • Bwill always be seen faster than one with an appointment
  • Cprefers mornings to afternoons
  • Dis never accepted by the clinic
  • Emust pay more than a patient with an appointment
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. When are walk-ins allowed? Only weekday mornings.
  2. Saturday is a weekend, so an appointment is required.
  3. Avoid options about speed or cost - not stated.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Exactly - no walk-ins on weekends.
If incorrect: D is too strong (walk-ins ARE accepted weekday mornings).

Answer: A. Walk-ins are allowed only weekday mornings; weekends require an appointment, so a walk-in cannot be seen Saturday. A is forced; the others add unsupported facts.

4You do — independent application

Application-first (SPOV2): commit to an answer, then reveal the explanation. Log every miss.

you_1 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Passage: All of the company's electric vehicles were built at its newest plant. The company's first plant, built in 1980, produces only gasoline vehicles. Question: The passage most strongly supports which of the following?
  • ANo electric vehicle was built at the 1980 plant.
  • BThe newest plant is larger than the 1980 plant.
  • CGasoline vehicles are cheaper to build.
  • DThe company stopped building gasoline vehicles.
  • EThe 1980 plant will be closed soon.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Electric vehicles all come from the newest plant, and the 1980 plant makes only gasoline vehicles, so no electric vehicle came from it. A is forced; the rest add facts.

you_2 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Passage: The festival is held outdoors and is canceled whenever it rains. This year the festival was held as scheduled. Question: It can be inferred from the passage that this year
  • Ait did not rain on the festival day
  • Bthe festival was more popular than last year
  • Cthe weather is usually sunny in the region
  • Dthe festival will be held indoors next year
  • Emore people attended than expected
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Rain forces cancellation; the festival was held, so by the contrapositive it did not rain. A is forced; the others are unsupported.

you_3 · you do (independent)hard

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Passage: Researchers noted that a certain frog species is found only in caves that stay near a constant cool temperature year-round. A newly surveyed cave has temperatures that swing widely between seasons. Question: The passage most strongly supports which of the following about the newly surveyed cave?
  • AThe frog species is unlikely to be found living there.
  • BThe cave is larger than the frogs' usual caves.
  • CThe frogs would thrive there if introduced.
  • DThe cave has no other animal species.
  • EThe temperature swings are caused by the frogs.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. The frog needs constant cool temperatures; this cave swings widely, so the frog is unlikely to live there. A is the supported inference; the others add or reverse facts.

5Mastery check

A topic is mastered at >=85% correct on new/held-out-style questions across >=2 spaced sessions with delay (PRD Section 9.2). Flashcard recall alone is not mastery; application under mixed, delayed conditions is the bar.

Threshold 0.85 · spaced sessions ≥ 2

  • >=85% correct on new/held-out-style items (not flashcard recall) per session
  • sustained across >=2 spaced sessions with a delay between them
  • per-topic memory surfaced only after >=20 graded reviews (PRD give-up rule)
  • per-topic performance surfaced only after >=8 application attempts (PRD give-up rule)

If not yet mastered: Not yet: route every miss to the required error log (tag why: content gap / reasoning flaw / timing / careless / trap), schedule spaced re-practice via topic-aware scheduling, then re-enter the we-do -> you-do loop before re-testing.
