Critical Reasoning: Evaluate the Argument

Topic id gmat::verbal::cr::evaluate · Domain Critical Reasoning · Prereqs identify an argument's assumption, understand strengthen and weaken · ~18 min

Learning objectives

  • Identify the question whose answer would most affect the argument's validity.
  • Use the 'variance test': a good evaluate answer helps either way it is resolved.
  • Avoid questions whose answers leave the conclusion unchanged.

Model: I do -> we do -> you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Knowing what strengthens and weakens an argument.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

No notes. 4 minutes.

  1. What makes a good 'evaluate' answer?Its answer would strengthen the argument one way and weaken it the other.
  2. If a question's answer doesn't change the conclusion either way, is it a good evaluate choice?No.
  3. Turn 'the plan will cut costs' into an evaluate target.Ask whether the savings exceed the plan's added expenses.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Recalls that an evaluate answer must be able to swing the argument both ways.
If students struggle: Have them literally answer the choice 'yes' then 'no' and see if the conclusion moves.

Prior-knowledge bridge

Strengthen and weaken each move the argument one direction. An evaluate question asks for the single fact that could move it EITHER direction - so you test a choice by answering it both ways and watching whether the conclusion shifts.

Learning intention

By the end you can select the question whose answer would most affect the argument, verified by the both-ways variance test.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can state what the argument concludes and assumes.
  • I can apply the both-ways test to a candidate question.
  • I can reject questions that don't move the conclusion.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
A hospital plans to shorten patient wait times by adding a second check-in clerk. The answer to which of the following would be most useful in evaluating whether the plan will work?
  • AWhether the check-in step, rather than a later step such as seeing a doctor, is what causes most of the waiting.
  • BWhether the second clerk will be paid the same as the first.
  • CWhether patients are satisfied with the current clerks.
  • DWhether the hospital has enough desks for two clerks.
  • EWhether wait times are longer on weekends.

Think-aloud

  1. Conclusion: a second clerk will cut wait times. Assumption: check-in is the bottleneck.
  2. Apply the both-ways test to A: bottleneck at check-in -> helps; bottleneck later -> useless.
  3. Since A's answer can strengthen or weaken, it's the key evaluation question.
  4. The others (pay, desks, satisfaction) don't change whether the plan works.

Correct: A. Test A both ways: if check-in is the bottleneck, a second clerk helps (strengthens); if the delay is later, a second clerk won't help (weakens). Because its answer swings the plan either way, A is the best evaluation target.

Takeaway. The best evaluate answer is the question whose two possible answers push the conclusion in opposite directions.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)medium
A publisher plans to raise profit by releasing its novels only as e-books. Which of the following would be most useful to determine in order to evaluate the plan?
  • AWhether most current print buyers would switch to e-books rather than buy from print competitors.
  • BWhether e-books can display cover art.
  • CHow long it takes to format an e-book.
  • DWhether the publisher's authors write fiction.
  • EWhether e-readers are dropping in price.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. What must be true for e-book-only to raise profit?
  2. Answer the choice 'yes' and 'no' - does the conclusion move?
  3. Only one choice changes the outcome either way.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right - buyer switching is the pivot the plan turns on.
If incorrect: Formatting time and cover art don't determine whether profit rises.

Answer: A. Both ways: if buyers switch to e-books, profit can rise; if they defect to print rivals, profit falls. Its answer swings the plan, so it is the key question.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
A farm plans to raise its apple yield by switching to a new irrigation system. The answer to which of the following would be most useful in evaluating the plan?
  • AWhether water supply, rather than something like soil quality, is what currently limits the yield.
  • BWhether the new system is quieter than the old one.
  • CWhether neighboring farms use the same system.
  • DWhether the apples are sold locally.
  • EWhether the system was installed by certified technicians.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. The plan assumes water is the bottleneck to yield.
  2. Test that assumption with the both-ways method.
  3. Which choice's answer could sink or save the plan?
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes - if water isn't the limit, better irrigation won't help.
If incorrect: Noise, installers, and sales location don't affect whether yield rises.

Answer: A. Both ways: if water is the limiting factor, better irrigation raises yield; if soil is the limit, it won't. The answer moves the conclusion either way, making A decisive.

4You do — independent application

Application-first (SPOV2): commit to an answer, then reveal the explanation. Log every miss.

you_1 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

A streaming service plans to gain subscribers by producing more original comedies. The answer to which of the following would be most useful in evaluating the plan?
  • AWhether the viewers the service hopes to attract choose a service based on its original comedies.
  • BWhether comedies cost less to produce than dramas.
  • CWhether the service currently offers any comedies.
  • DWhether competitors also produce comedies.
  • EWhether comedies win more awards than dramas.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. Both ways: if target viewers pick services for comedies, the plan can add subscribers; if not, it won't. The answer swings the conclusion, so A is the key question.
