Reading Comprehension: Main Idea

Topic id gmat::verbal::rc::main_idea · Domain Reading Comprehension · Prereqs read a short passage for its overall point, tell a main point from a supporting detail · ~18 min

Learning objectives

  • State the passage's primary purpose in one sentence before reading the choices.
  • Choose the option that covers the WHOLE passage, not just one paragraph.
  • Reject options that are too narrow, too broad, or not discussed.

Model: I do -> we do -> you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Summarizing a paragraph in a single sentence.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

No notes. 4 minutes.

  1. What is a passage's 'primary purpose'?The main job the whole passage does (e.g. explain, argue, describe).
  2. Is a detail from one sentence usually the main idea?No - the main idea covers the whole passage.
  3. Why predict the main idea before reading choices?To avoid being pulled by narrow but tempting options.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Recalls the difference between a whole-passage point and a single detail.
If students struggle: Have them write a one-line summary of the passage first, then match it to an option.

Prior-knowledge bridge

You can summarize a paragraph. A main-idea question just scales that up: capture what the ENTIRE passage is doing in one sentence, then pick the choice that matches that sentence - not a true-but-narrow detail.

Learning intention

By the end you can state a passage's primary purpose and select the option that matches the whole passage.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can summarize a passage's overall point in one sentence.
  • I can reject options that are too narrow or not discussed.
  • I can pick the option that spans the whole passage.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Passage: The invention of cheap aluminum in the late nineteenth century transformed industries far beyond the obvious. Once as costly as silver, aluminum became affordable after a new electrolytic process was devised, and it soon reshaped packaging, transportation, and construction. Question: The primary purpose of the passage is to
  • Adescribe how a technological change had wide-ranging effects
  • Bargue that aluminum is more useful than silver
  • Cexplain the chemistry of the electrolytic process in detail
  • Dcriticize nineteenth-century packaging methods
  • Epredict future uses of aluminum in construction

Think-aloud

  1. One-line summary: a new process made aluminum cheap, and that changed many industries.
  2. Predict: purpose = show a technological change had broad effects.
  3. Match to A; reject B/C/D/E as narrow or unstated.

Correct: A. The passage says cheap aluminum 'reshaped packaging, transportation, and construction' - a wide-ranging effect. A covers the whole passage; the others are narrow or not stated.

Takeaway. Predict the whole-passage point first, then pick the option that matches it.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)medium
Passage: Coral reefs occupy a tiny fraction of the ocean floor, yet they shelter a quarter of all marine species. Scientists studying reef resilience have found that reefs exposed to mild, occasional heat stress sometimes withstand later, severe heat waves better than reefs that had been fully protected. Question: The passage is primarily concerned with
  • Aa surprising finding about what helps reefs survive heat
  • Bproving that reefs cover very little of the ocean floor
  • Clisting the species that live on coral reefs
  • Darguing that reefs should be fully protected from all stress
  • Edescribing how heat waves form in the ocean
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. What is the passage's most important, whole-passage point?
  2. The reef-area fact is just a setup detail.
  3. Watch for an option that contradicts the finding (full protection).
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right - the resilience finding is the main point.
If incorrect: The area statistic and species count are supporting details, not the main idea.

Answer: A. The passage's point is the surprising resilience finding (mild prior stress can help). A matches the whole passage; the others are narrow or contradicted.

we_2 · we do (guided)easy
Passage: The invention of cheap aluminum in the late nineteenth century transformed industries far beyond the obvious. Once as costly as silver, aluminum became affordable after a new electrolytic process was devised, and it soon reshaped packaging, transportation, and construction. Question: Which of the following best expresses the main idea of the passage?
  • AA drop in aluminum's cost led it to affect many industries.
  • BAluminum was once as expensive as silver.
  • CThe electrolytic process is difficult to describe.
  • DPackaging improved more than transportation did.
  • EConstruction depends entirely on aluminum.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Pick the sentence that could headline the whole passage.
  2. A single fact (once costly as silver) is too narrow.
  3. Avoid extreme wording (entirely).
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes - broad impact from a cost drop.
If incorrect: B is true but narrow; the main idea must span the passage.

Answer: A. The whole passage is about cheap aluminum's broad impact. A states that; B is a setup detail, and C/D/E are unstated or overstated.

4You do — independent application

Application-first (SPOV2): commit to an answer, then reveal the explanation. Log every miss.

you_1 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Passage: Before refrigerated railcars, most fresh meat was slaughtered near cities. Refrigerated cars let packers centralize slaughter in a few large plants and ship dressed meat long distances, lowering costs but drawing complaints from local butchers. Question: The primary purpose of the passage is to
  • Aexplain how a new technology changed an industry and note a reaction to it
  • Bargue that local butchers produced better meat
  • Cdescribe the mechanical design of refrigerated railcars
  • Dprove that centralized slaughter was a mistake
  • Etrace the history of cities near railways
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. The passage explains the change (refrigerated cars centralized slaughter) and notes a reaction (butchers' complaints). A captures both; the others are narrow or unstated.

you_2 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Passage: Many assume bird migration is triggered mainly by falling temperature. Studies of caged birds, however, show that even in constant warmth the birds grow restless at the usual migration time, suggesting an internal calendar plays a large role. Question: Which of the following best states the main point of the passage?
  • AAn internal timing mechanism, not just temperature, drives migration.
  • BCaged birds behave exactly like wild birds.
  • CTemperature has no effect on birds at all.
  • DBird migration occurs at the same time every year everywhere.
  • EStudying caged birds is the only way to understand migration.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. The study shows restlessness even in constant warmth, pointing to an internal calendar. A states the main point; the others overstate or contradict.

5Mastery check

A topic is mastered at >=85% correct on new/held-out-style questions across >=2 spaced sessions with delay (PRD Section 9.2). Flashcard recall alone is not mastery; application under mixed, delayed conditions is the bar.

Threshold 0.85 · spaced sessions ≥ 2

  • per-topic memory surfaced only after >=20 graded reviews (PRD give-up rule)
