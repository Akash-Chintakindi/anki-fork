Word Problems: Rate, Work, Mixture & Interest

Topic id gmat::quant::algebra::word_problems · Domain Algebra · Prereqs linear equations, ratios & proportions, percents · ~26 min

Learning objectives

  • Use distance = rate × time, including total-time and relative-speed setups.
  • Solve work problems by adding rates (jobs per unit time).
  • Set up mixture/concentration problems by tracking the pure quantity.
  • Compute simple interest with I = P·r·t.

Model: I do → we do → you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Linear equations, rates/ratios, and percents.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

From memory, no calculator. 4 minutes.

  1. A car goes 180 miles in 3 hours. What is its speed?60 mph.
  2. Find distance if rate = 50 mph and time = 4 h.200 miles (d = rt).
  3. If a worker finishes a job in 6 hours, what fraction is done in 1 hour?1/6.
  4. 5% simple interest on $200 for 1 year = ?$10.
  5. Two cars approach each other at 40 and 60 mph. How fast does the gap close?100 mph — speeds add when moving toward each other.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Retrieves D = rt, work-as-rate, percent-of, and relative speed — the four setups behind today's classic word-problem types.
If students struggle: If D = rt is shaky, drill the triangle (cover the unknown to get the formula) before composite problems.

Prior-knowledge bridge

Every word problem today is a translation exercise built on one relationship you already know: rate × time = amount (miles, jobs, dollars of interest). The art is choosing a variable, writing one honest equation, and solving it with your linear-equation skills. Q5's relative-speed idea unlocks the trickier travel problems.

Learning intention

By the end you can translate rate, work, mixture, and interest scenarios into equations and solve them.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can set up and solve distance = rate × time problems, including total time.
  • I can add work rates to find combined or individual times.
  • I can track the pure quantity in a mixture and compute simple interest.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Pipe A fills a tank in 4 hours and pipe B fills the same tank in 6 hours. Working together, how long do they take to fill the tank?
  • A2.4 hours
  • B5 hours
  • C2 hours
  • D3 hours
  • E10 hours

Think-aloud

  1. Convert each time to a rate: A does 1/4 tank per hour, B does 1/6.
  2. Working together, rates ADD: 1/4 + 1/6 = 5/12 tank per hour.
  3. Time = total work ÷ rate = 1 ÷ (5/12) = 12/5 = 2.4 hours (2 h 24 min).

Correct: A. Add rates: 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12 of the tank per hour. Time = 1 ÷ (5/12) = 12/5 = 2.4 hours.

Takeaway. Work problems: add RATES (jobs per hour), never times. Combined time = 1 ÷ (sum of rates).

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)easy
A cyclist rides 60 miles at a steady 15 mph. How long does the trip take?
  • A3 hours
  • B4 hours
  • C5 hours
  • D45 hours
  • E900 hours
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Rearrange d = rt to t = d/r.
  2. 60 ÷ 15.
  3. = 4 hours.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right — 4 hours.
If incorrect: Divide distance by rate; don't multiply.

Answer: B. time = distance ÷ rate = 60 ÷ 15 = 4 hours.

we_2 · we do (guided)easy
What is the simple interest on $500 at 6% per year for 2 years?
  • A$30
  • B$60
  • C$530
  • D$560
  • E$600
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Simple interest I = principal × rate × time.
  2. 500 × 0.06 = 30 per year.
  3. × 2 years = 60.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes — $60 of interest.
If incorrect: The question asks for interest only, not the new balance ($560).

Answer: B. I = P·r·t = 500 × 0.06 × 2 = $60.

A car travels 150 miles at 50 mph, then 120 miles at 40 mph. What is the total travel time?
  • A5 hours
  • B5.5 hours
  • C6 hours
  • D6.5 hours

Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: C. 150/50 = 3 hours and 120/40 = 3 hours; total = 6 hours.

you_2 · you do (independent) · target 120shard

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Two workers together paint a fence in 3 hours. One of them alone would take 5 hours. How long would the other take alone?
  • A2 hours
  • B7.5 hours
  • C8 hours
  • D4 hours
  • E15 hours
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: B. Combined rate 1/3; one is 1/5; the other = 1/3 − 1/5 = 2/15 fence/hour → time = 15/2 = 7.5 hours.

you_3 · you do (independent) · target 120shard

Application-first — attempt before revealing

How many liters of pure water must be added to 10 liters of a 40% salt solution to dilute it to a 25% solution?
  • A4
  • B5
  • C6
  • D8
  • E15
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: C. Salt stays 0.40 × 10 = 4 L. Need 4/(10 + x) = 0.25 → 10 + x = 16 → x = 6 liters.

you_4 · you do (independent) · target 120shard

Application-first — attempt before revealing

A boat travels 30 miles downstream in 2 hours and returns the same 30 miles upstream in 3 hours. What is the speed of the boat in still water?
  • A10 mph
  • B11 mph
  • C12.5 mph
  • D13 mph
  • E15 mph
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: C. Downstream b + c = 30/2 = 15; upstream b − c = 30/3 = 10. Adding: 2b = 25, so b = 12.5 mph.

  • A10
  • B12
