Data Insights: Multi-Source Reasoning

Topic id gmat::di::reasoning::multi_source_reasoning · Domain Data Insights · Prereqs read a short text or table for a specific fact, combine two given facts to reach a conclusion · ~18 min

Learning objectives

  • Identify which source holds each fact a question needs.
  • Combine facts across two or three sources to reach the answer.
  • Ignore sources that do not bear on the question.

Model: I do -> we do -> you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Reading a text or table for a fact and combining two facts into a conclusion.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

No notes. Answer from memory in 4 minutes.

  1. Text A: 'the store opens at 9 a.m.' Text B: 'deliveries arrive 2 hours after opening.' When do deliveries arrive?11 a.m. (9 + 2).
  2. A price list shows $15 and a coupon gives 10% off. What is the paid price?$13.50.
  3. A question asks only about cost. Must you use a source that lists only dates?No - use only the sources carrying the facts you need.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Recalls cross-referencing two facts and using only the sources a question needs - the core moves of Multi-Source Reasoning.
If students struggle: Have them underline the one fact each source contributes before answering.

Prior-knowledge bridge

You can pull a fact from a text or table and combine two facts into a conclusion. Multi-Source Reasoning gives you two or three short sources (like tabs) and asks a question whose answer usually lives in the COMBINATION of them. Your job is to find which source holds each needed fact, ignore the rest, and put the pieces together.

Learning intention

By the end you can locate the relevant facts across two or three sources and combine them to answer a question, ignoring any source that does not bear on it.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I identify exactly what the question is asking for.
  • I find which source holds each fact I need.
  • I combine those facts (and skip irrelevant sources) to reach the answer.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Source 1 - Notice: The Riverside Community Center offers Pottery, Painting, and Weaving classes. Each class session is listed at $15. Source 2 - Email: Members pay 20% less than the listed price for every session. Question: A member attends one Pottery session and one Painting session. What total does she pay?
  • A$24
  • B$21
  • C$27
  • D$30
  • E$12

Think-aloud

  1. The question asks the total a member pays for two sessions - I need the price and the member rule.
  2. Source 1 gives the listed price: $15 per session.
  3. Source 2 gives the member rule: 20% less, so $15 - $3 = $12 per session.
  4. Two sessions at the member price: 2 x $12 = $24.
  5. That is option A; $30 ignores the discount and $27 discounts only one session.

Correct: A. The question needs the price (Source 1) and the member rule (Source 2). Listed price is $15 per session; members pay 20% less, so $12 per session. Two sessions at the member price: 2 x $12 = $24, option A. $30 ignores the discount, and $27 discounts only one session.

Takeaway. In Multi-Source Reasoning the answer usually comes from combining sources: find which source holds each needed fact, apply any rule, and integrate - do not answer from a single source when another one modifies it.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)medium
Source 1 - Schedule: Shuttle A leaves the North Lot at 9:00 a.m.; Shuttle B leaves the South Lot at 9:20 a.m. Each shuttle takes 20 minutes to reach campus. Source 2 - Notice: The North Lot is closed all week for construction. Source 3 - Email: Dr. Lee must be on campus by 9:45 a.m. Question: Which shuttle can Dr. Lee take and still arrive on time?
  • AShuttle A from the North Lot.
  • BShuttle B from the South Lot.
  • CEither shuttle works.
  • DShuttle A from the South Lot.
  • ENeither shuttle arrives by 9:45 a.m.
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. First, which lots are usable? Check the closure notice.
  2. The North Lot is closed, so any shuttle from it is out.
  3. For the remaining shuttle, add its 20-minute ride to its departure and compare with the 9:45 deadline.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right - the North Lot is closed, and Shuttle B (9:20 + 20 min = 9:40) beats the 9:45 deadline.
If incorrect: Combine all three sources: the closure rules out the North Lot shuttle, then check the other shuttle's arrival against 9:45.

Answer: B. Source 2 closes the North Lot, so Shuttle A is out. Shuttle B leaves the South Lot at 9:20 and takes 20 minutes, arriving 9:40 - before the 9:45 deadline in Source 3. So the answer is Shuttle B from the South Lot, option B.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
Source 1 - Room rates: Standard $90 per night; Deluxe $130 per night; Suite $180 per night. Source 2 - Policy: Any stay of 3 or more nights receives $20 off per night on any room. Question: A guest books a Deluxe room for 3 nights. What is the total room cost?
  • A$270
  • B$310
  • C$330
  • D$370
  • E$390
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Find the Deluxe nightly rate in the price table.
  2. Check the discount policy - does a 3-night stay qualify?
  3. Apply the per-night discount, then multiply by the number of nights.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Exactly - $130 - $20 = $110 per night, times 3 nights = $330.
If incorrect: The 3-night stay earns the discount on EVERY night; apply it to all three, then total.

Answer: C. Source 1 lists Deluxe at $130 per night. Source 2 gives $20 off per night for stays of 3 or more nights, so $110 per night. Three nights: 3 x $110 = $330, option C. $390 forgets the discount; $370 applies it to only one night.

4You do — independent application

Application-first (SPOV2): commit to an answer, then reveal the explanation. Log every miss.

you_1 · you do (independent)easy

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Source 1 - Email: Our book club has 5 members, and we want one copy of the new novel for each member. Source 2 - Store listing: The novel costs $18 in hardcover and $11 in paperback. Source 3 - Note: The treasury has $70, and everyone must receive the same format. Question: Which format can the club afford for all five members?
  • AHardcover only.
  • BEither format.
  • CNeither format.
  • DPaperback only.
  • EHardcover for three members and paperback for two.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: D. Hardcover for all five: 5 x $18 = $90, which is more than the $70 budget - unaffordable. Paperback for all five: 5 x $11 = $55, within budget - affordable. Source 3 requires the same format for everyone, so the split option is out. The answer is paperback only, option D.

you_2 · you do (independent)hard

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Source 1 - Timetable: Train X arrives at Central Station at 2:10 p.m. Source 2 - Bus notice: The museum bus leaves Central Station every 30 minutes starting at 2:00 p.m. (2:00, 2:30, 3:00, and so on); the ride takes 25 minutes. Source 3 - Email: I need to reach the museum by 3:00 p.m. Question: If the traveler takes Train X, what is the earliest she can arrive at the museum?
  • A2:35 p.m.
  • B3:00 p.m.
  • C2:30 p.m.
  • D3:25 p.m.
  • E2:55 p.m.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: E. Train X arrives at 2:10, so the first catchable bus is the 2:30 (the 2:00 has already left). 2:30 plus the 25-minute ride is 2:55 p.m., which meets the 3:00 deadline. The answer is 2:55 p.m., option E. 2:35 wrongly assumes a bus leaves the moment she arrives.

you_3 · you do (independent)medium

Application-first — attempt before revealing

Source 1 - Inventory: Warehouse A holds 120 units; Warehouse B holds 90 units of the product. Source 2 - Order: A customer orders 150 units, to ship from a single warehouse if possible, otherwise split between warehouses. Source 3 - Policy: A split shipment (two warehouses) adds a $25 fee; single-warehouse shipping is free. Question: Will the order incur the split-shipment fee, and why?
  • AYes; neither warehouse alone holds 150 units, so the order must be split.
  • BNo; Warehouse A alone can fill the order.
  • CNo; Warehouse B alone can fill the order.
  • DNo; the order can be split with no fee.
  • EYes; the customer asked to split the shipment to save money.
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: A. The order is 150 units. Warehouse A holds 120 and Warehouse B holds 90, so neither can fill 150 alone; the order must ship from both, which triggers the $25 split-shipment fee (Source 3). The answer is A.

5Mastery check

A topic is mastered at >=85% correct on new/held-out-style questions across >=2 spaced sessions with delay (PRD Section 9.2). Flashcard recall alone is not mastery; application under mixed, delayed conditions is the bar.

Threshold 0.85 · spaced sessions ≥ 2

  • >=85% correct on new/held-out-style items (not flashcard recall) per session
  • sustained across >=2 spaced sessions with a delay between them
  • per-topic memory surfaced only after >=20 graded reviews (PRD give-up rule)
  • per-topic performance surfaced only after >=8 application attempts (PRD give-up rule)

If not yet mastered: Not yet: route every miss to the required error log (tag why: content gap / reasoning flaw / timing / careless / trap), schedule spaced re-practice via topic-aware scheduling, then re-enter the we-do -> you-do loop before re-testing.
