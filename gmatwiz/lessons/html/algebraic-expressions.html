Algebraic Expressions & Exponent Rules

Topic id gmat::quant::algebra::algebraic_expressions · Domain Algebra · Prereqs exponents & roots, number properties · ~22 min

Learning objectives

  • Combine like terms and distribute correctly, watching signs.
  • Multiply binomials with FOIL and recognize special products.
  • Factor out a GCF and factor a difference of squares.
  • Evaluate expressions by substitution and use identities like (a+b)^2.

Model: I do → we do → you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Exponent rules and the order of operations.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

From memory, no calculator. 4 minutes.

  1. Simplify 3x + 5x − 2x.6x.
  2. Expand 2(x + 4).2x + 8.
  3. Simplify x^2 · x^3.x^5.
  4. Evaluate 2a + 3 when a = 4.11.
  5. Why can't 3x + 2y be simplified to 5xy?They are unlike terms — only terms with the same variable part combine.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Retrieves like terms, distribution, the product rule, and substitution — the moves you'll chain into FOIL and factoring today.
If students struggle: If distribution slips, model the sign trap: −2(x − 5) = −2x + 10, not −2x − 10.

Prior-knowledge bridge

Q5 pins down the one rule beginners break: you can only combine like terms. Distribution and FOIL are just organized ways to create like terms and then collect them; factoring is running that machine in reverse. Today you'll expand, factor, and evaluate — the algebra plumbing behind every equation later in the course.

Learning intention

By the end you can expand products with FOIL, factor out common factors and differences of squares, and evaluate expressions accurately.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can distribute a negative without sign errors.
  • I can multiply two binomials and collect the middle term.
  • I can factor out a GCF and recognize a^2 − b^2.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
Expand and simplify: (x + 3)(x − 5).
  • Ax^2 − 2x − 15
  • Bx^2 + 2x − 15
  • Cx^2 − 8x − 15
  • Dx^2 − 2x + 15
  • Ex^2 − 15

Think-aloud

  1. First: x · x = x^2.
  2. Outer: x · (−5) = −5x. Inner: 3 · x = 3x.
  3. Last: 3 · (−5) = −15.
  4. Combine the middle terms: −5x + 3x = −2x, giving x^2 − 2x − 15.

Correct: A. FOIL then combine: x·x + x·(−5) + 3·x + 3·(−5) = x^2 − 5x + 3x − 15 = x^2 − 2x − 15.

Takeaway. FOIL: multiply every term in the first factor by every term in the second, then collect the like (middle) terms.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)easy
Simplify: 4(2x − 3) − 2(x − 5).
  • A6x − 2
  • B6x − 22
  • C6x + 2
  • D10x − 2
  • E6x − 7
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Distribute both groups first.
  2. Mind the sign: −2(x − 5) = −2x + 10.
  3. Combine x-terms and constants separately.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right — 6x − 2.
If incorrect: The −2 multiplies BOTH terms, turning −5 into +10. Re-do that distribution.

Answer: A. Distribute: 8x − 12 − 2x + 10. Combine: (8x − 2x) + (−12 + 10) = 6x − 2.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
Factor completely: 6x^2 + 9x.
  • A3x(2x + 3)
  • B3(2x^2 + 3x)
  • Cx(6x + 9)
  • D3x(2x + 9)
  • E6x(x + 3)
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. What is the greatest common factor of 6x^2 and 9x?
  2. Numbers share 3; both terms share an x → 3x.
  3. 6x^2 ÷ 3x = 2x and 9x ÷ 3x = 3.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes — 3x(2x + 3), fully factored.
If incorrect: Pull out the FULL common factor (3x), not just 3 or just x.

Answer: A. GCF of 6x^2 and 9x is 3x: 6x^2 + 9x = 3x(2x + 3).

5Mastery check

A topic is mastered at >=85% correct on new/held-out-style questions across >=2 spaced sessions with delay (PRD Section 9.2). Flashcard recall alone is not mastery; application under mixed, delayed conditions is the bar.

Threshold 0.85 · spaced sessions ≥ 2

  • sustained across >=2 spaced sessions with a delay between them
  • per-topic memory surfaced only after >=20 graded reviews (PRD give-up rule)

If not yet mastered: Not yet: route every miss to the required error log (tag why: content gap / reasoning flaw / timing / careless / trap), schedule spaced re-practice via topic-aware scheduling, then re-enter the we-do -> you-do loop before re-testing.
