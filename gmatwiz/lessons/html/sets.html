Sets & Venn Diagrams

Topic id gmat::quant::arithmetic::sets · Domain Arithmetic · Prereqs addition & subtraction, percents (for proportion-of-group overlaps) · ~22 min

Learning objectives

  • Use union, intersection, and complement correctly.
  • Apply the two-set formula |A∪B| = |A| + |B| − |A∩B| and account for 'neither'.
  • Translate overlap word problems into a Venn structure (only-A, only-B, both, neither).
  • Find 'only one group' counts from totals and overlaps.

Model: I do → we do → you do · application-first (explanations revealed after an attempt) · Rosenshine (daily review), Archer (I-do/we-do/you-do), SPOV2 application-first.

1Opening — retrieval & bridge

Builds on: Adding and subtracting counts; reading 'and/or'.

Do Now · from memory, no notes

From memory, no calculator. 4 minutes.

  1. A = {1,2,3,4}, B = {3,4,5}. List A ∩ B.{3, 4}.
  2. For the same sets, list A ∪ B.{1, 2, 3, 4, 5}.
  3. How many elements are in that A ∪ B?5.
  4. Of 30 students, 18 play a sport. How many do not?12 (the complement).
  5. If 18 like tea and 15 like coffee in a class of 30, why isn't the total 33?People who like BOTH are counted twice; the overlap is double-counted.

Retrieval starter (5 min). Retrieves union/intersection/complement and exposes the double-counting problem that inclusion–exclusion fixes.
If students struggle: If union vs intersection blurs, anchor: 'or' = union (everything), 'and' = intersection (overlap only).

Prior-knowledge bridge

Q5 names today's whole idea: when you add two group sizes, the people in both get counted twice. The fix is to subtract the overlap once — that's the two-set formula. Combined with 'neither', it turns any overlap word problem into a quick arithmetic setup.

Learning intention

By the end you can solve two-set overlap problems with |A∪B| = |A| + |B| − |A∩B|, include a 'neither' group, and extract 'only-A' counts.

You'll know you've got it when:

  • I can find the overlap when given two group sizes and the union (or total and neither).
  • I can find how many are in exactly one group.
  • I can lay out only-A / only-B / both / neither and have them sum to the total.

2I do — worked example

Watch the reasoning modeled end to end before you try.

I DO · worked examplemedium
In a group of 40 people, 25 speak French, 18 speak Spanish, and 7 speak both languages. How many speak neither?
  • A2
  • B4
  • C6
  • D11
  • E15

Think-aloud

  1. First find how many speak at least one language (the union).
  2. |F ∪ S| = |F| + |S| − |both| = 25 + 18 − 7 = 36.
  3. Neither = total − union = 40 − 36 = 4.

Correct: B. Speakers of at least one = 25 + 18 − 7 = 36. Neither = total − union = 40 − 36 = 4.

Takeaway. Total = only-A + only-B + both + neither; equivalently Total = |A| + |B| − |both| + neither.

3We do — guided practice

Scaffolds and immediate feedback are one click away. Fade them as you gain confidence.

we_1 · we do (guided)easy
In a class of 30, 20 study math and 14 study physics; 8 study both. How many study at least one of the two?
  • A22
  • B24
  • C26
  • D28
  • E34
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Add the two groups, then remove the overlap once.
  2. 20 + 14 = 34.
  3. Subtract both (8): 34 − 8 = 26.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Right — subtracting the overlap once avoids double counting.
If incorrect: 34 double-counts the 8 who do both; subtract them once.

Answer: C. |M ∪ P| = 20 + 14 − 8 = 26.

we_2 · we do (guided)medium
Of 50 people, 30 own a car, 25 own a bike, and 10 own neither. How many own both?
  • A5
  • B10
  • C15
  • D20
  • E25
Scaffold hints (peek only if stuck)
  1. Find 'at least one' first: total − neither = 50 − 10 = 40.
  2. Now use 40 = 30 + 25 − both.
  3. both = 55 − 40.
Immediate feedback & explanation

If correct: Yes — 15 own both.
If incorrect: Subtract 'neither' from the total to get the union, then solve for the overlap.

Answer: C. Own at least one = 50 − 10 = 40. Then 40 = 30 + 25 − both, so both = 55 − 40 = 15.

In a survey of 60 people, 35 like apples and 40 like bananas. Every person likes at least one. How many like both?
  • A5
  • B10
  • C15
  • D20
  • E25
Reveal explanation (after your attempt)

Answer: C. Union = 60 (everyone likes at least one), so both = 35 + 40 − 60 = 15.
