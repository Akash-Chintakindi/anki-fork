A rectangular garden is 3 meters longer than it is wide. Its area is 40 square meters. What is the width, in meters?

The explanation is sealed until you answer. Lessons build confidence; the attempt is what builds competence.

Explanation

Let the width be w, so the length is w + 3. Area gives w(w + 3) = 40, i.e. w² + 3w − 40 = 0.

Factor: (w − 5)(w + 8) = 0, so w = 5 or w = −8. A width can’t be negative, so w = 5. Check: 5 × 8 = 40. ✓

Trap A (“4”) comes from solving w(w+3)=40 by guessing factors of 40 without forming the quadratic.
